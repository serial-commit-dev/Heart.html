Artery

  • Carries blood *away* from the heart (mostly oxygenated).
  • Contains oxygenated blood (pure blood).
  • Thick and elastic walls.
  • Blood flows with high pressure and high speed.

Capillary

  • Thin vessels connecting veins and arteries.
  • Wall formed of a single layer of cells.
  • Minute pores on the walls for gas and nutrient exchange.
  • Valves are absent.
  • Blood flows with low speed and low pressure.
  • Tissue fluid and cells are present.

Veins
  • Carries blood *to* the heart (mostly deoxygenated).
  • Contains deoxygenated blood (impure blood).
  • Thin wall.
  • Valves are present.
  • Blood flows with low speed and low pressure.

Tissue Fluid

Tissue fluid, also known as interstitial fluid, surrounds cells and fills spaces between capillaries. It's derived from blood plasma.

Anatomy of a Heart

How the Heart Functions

  • Superior Vena Cava: Carries deoxygenated blood from the upper body to the heart.
  • Inferior Vena Cava: Carries deoxygenated blood from the lower body to the heart.
  • Right Atrium: Receives blood from both Vena Cavae.
  • Tricuspid Valve: Allows blood flow from the Right Atrium to the Right Ventricle.
  • Right Ventricle: Pumps blood to the lungs via the Pulmonary Artery.
  • Pulmonary Artery: Transports deoxygenated blood to the lungs for oxygenation.
  • Pulmonary Vein: Returns oxygenated blood from the lungs to the heart (Left Atrium).
  • Left Atrium: Receives oxygenated blood from the Pulmonary Vein.
  • Left Ventricle: Pumps oxygenated blood to the body via the Aorta.
  • Aorta: The body's largest artery; distributes oxygenated blood throughout the body.

More about the Heart

  • The time required for the heart to make one full heartbeat is just 0.8 seconds
  • The heart is a muscle: It's the strongest muscle in the body, constantly working.
  • Electrical impulses: The heart's rhythm is controlled by electrical impulses generated within the heart itself.
  • Four chambers: The heart has four chambers: two atria (receiving chambers) and two ventricles (pumping chambers).
  • Valves: One-way valves prevent blood from flowing backward.
  • Coronary arteries: These arteries supply the heart muscle with blood and oxygen.
