Heart Anatomy and Function

Understanding Arteries, Capillaries, and Veins

The above illustration represents arteries, capillaries, and veins.

Arteries

  • Carries blood away from the heart.
  • Contains oxygenated blood (except for pulmonary arteries).
  • Thick and elastic walls.
  • Blood flows with high pressure and speed.

Capillaries

  • Thin vessels that connect veins and arteries.
  • Wall formed of a single layer of cells.
  • Minute pores on the walls.
  • Valves are absent.
  • Blood flows with low speed and pressure.
  • Tissue fluid is present.

Veins

  • Carries blood to the heart.
  • Contains deoxygenated blood (except for pulmonary veins).
  • Thin walls.
  • Valves are present.
  • Blood flows with low speed and pressure.

Tissue Fluid

Tissue fluid, also known as interstitial fluid, surrounds cells and fills the spaces between capillaries. It's a solution derived from blood plasma.

Anatomy of the Heart

How the Heart Functions:

  • Superior Vena Cava: Carries deoxygenated blood from the upper body to the heart.
  • Inferior Vena Cava: Carries deoxygenated blood from the lower body to the heart.
  • Right Atrium: Receives blood from both Vena Cavae.
  • Tricuspid Valve: Allows blood flow from the Right Atrium to the Right Ventricle.
  • Right Ventricle: Pumps blood to the lungs via the Pulmonary Artery.
  • Pulmonary Artery: Transports deoxygenated blood to the lungs for oxygenation.
  • Pulmonary Vein: Returns oxygenated blood to the heart.
